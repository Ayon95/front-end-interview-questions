Block vs inline vs inline-block display

Paragraphs are block elements. Block elements occupy the entire horizontal space of their parent container.

Paragraphs are block elements. Block elements occupy the entire horizontal space of their parent container.

This paragraph contains some text wrapped in span tag. These span elements have inlinedisplay. Inline elements do not occupy the entire horizontal space of their parent container. They do not cause a line break at the end. They are placed one after another horizontally. As you can see, vertical padding or margins will not push away surrounding elements or content. Furthermore, you cannot set an explicit width or height on an inline element. Transforms also do not work on inline elements. Hover over me!

The span elements in this paragraph have been given inlineblockdisplay. Inline block elements are a combination of block and inline elements. Like inline elements, they do not occupy the full width of their parent container. However, like block elements, vertical margins and padding will push away surrounding elements. You can also set explicit width and height. You can also apply transforms. Hover over me!
